# Changelog

All notable milestones for the EvoRadar engine and platform.

---

## 2026-03-26
**v8 engine live on evoradar.ai** — 1,650+ ideas evaluated, 74 active genes, 3 generation flavors running daily. Public showcase repository created.

## 2026-03-25
**Signal matrix expansion** — 248 signals across 25 industries and 5 regions. Introduced G074 Variable Boldness gene, allowing the engine to dynamically adjust exploration aggressiveness per run.

## 2026-03-20
**v8 triple-run system launched** — Engine now runs all three flavors (Capability, Painpoint, Crossdomain) daily in a single automated pipeline. Combined checkpoint system ensures consistency across flavors.

## 2026-03-14
**Domain migration to evoradar.ai** — Migrated from subdomain to dedicated domain. Full auth reconfiguration (PKCE, OAuth, email templates). DNS, SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured.

## 2026-03-09
**v8 AI-native architecture redesign** — Fundamental shift from scripted pipeline to autonomous thinking engine. The engine no longer follows a fixed evaluation template — it reasons freely, then structures its conclusions. Self-evolution flywheel: genes mutate based on outcomes, not manual tuning.

## 2026-03-01
**v7.5 backfill and reality check** — Introduced L6 Reality Check pass after discovering that 4/4 HOT-scoring ideas failed founder review. Engine now actively checks for existing competitors and structural market barriers (Pastor Effect detection).

## 2026-02
**v7.4 scoring overhaul** — Switched to geometric mean scoring (single catastrophic weakness kills the idea). Discovered and fixed HOT threshold bug — threshold was trivially easy to cross with new scale. Raised from 5.0 to 7.0, then to 8.0.

## 2026-01
**v7.0–v7.3** — First real output at scale. 2,000+ ideas generated across multiple runs. Kill rate evolved from 53% (v7.0) to 90% (v7.3 Run 3). Engine learned to be ruthless.

## 2025 (pre-public)
**v1–v6** — Five complete internal rewrites before the engine produced anything worth sharing. v1 generated 60 ideas and killed zero of them — every single one was an "AI fills a form" pattern. Each version taught the engine what not to do.

---

*The engine evolves continuously. This changelog captures major milestones only.*
